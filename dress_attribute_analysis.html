STRATEGIC ANALYSIS DOSSIER (DRESSES)
February 2025

1. Opening Summary

Key insights from the data:

  • Only objective attributes were included—no brand-language or ambiguous terms.
  • Competitors in scope: 11 brands – Bec & Bridge, Camilla and Marc, Farm Rio, Frankie Shop, Ganni, House of CB, Me+Em, Meshki, Reformation, Rixo, Self Portrait.
  • Total SKUs considered: 3,407.
  • Top 5 competitor attributes R&B does not mention: Cotton, Bra Friendly/Modesty, Strapless, Pleated.

Important Considerations:

  • If a brand doesn’t mention an attribute, it doesn’t necessarily mean they don’t have it—it just wasn’t explicitly stated in the product description.
  • When a brand includes an attribute in its product details, it is a clear confirmation that they offer it.
  • Comparing what brands highlight reveals their priorities, exposing messaging gaps and positioning differences. This helps R&B refine its product focus and improve tagging accuracy.

2. Category Leadership & Key Strengths

Market position and distinctive attributes

  • Rat & Boa co-leads slip dress market at 16.1% share (tied with Meshki)
  • R&B mentions slip dresses in 29.9% of product descriptions vs. 3.3% competitor average
  • Significant advantage in all printed designs (49.5% vs. 7.8% competitor average)
    • Within prints category: Animal (7.5%), Abstract (5.6%), Floral (10.3%), Other prints (26.1%)
    • Some competitors have higher specific print usage (e.g., Ganni's animal prints at 20.5%), but within smaller overall print offerings

5. Strategic Opportunities

Recommendations to maintain brand DNA while capturing new market segments

  1. Maintain Category Leadership
    • Continue emphasis on slip dresses while enhancing printed and animal print offerings
    • Leverage unique abstract pattern dominance for differentiation
  2. Balanced Product Messaging
    • Introduce "bra-friendly" and "lined" descriptors for select pieces
    • Develop more modest styles while maintaining sensual brand aesthetic
    • Highlight premium materials more consistently (silk, georgette)
  3. Differentiation Strategy
    • Preserve core sensual aesthetic while strategically expanding modest options
    • Target specific occasions where modesty is valued without compromising brand DNA

Note: When attributes appear in product descriptions, it confirms their presence in the product. However, absence from descriptions does not necessarily mean the feature is missing—only that it wasn't highlighted in marketing materials. High usage percentages likely underrepresent actual prevalence, as some instances may not be mentioned. Confidence is highest for objective attributes like dress length (midi, maxi) and fabric composition (cotton, silk), while more subjective features like sheerness may be inconsistently mentioned across brands.
