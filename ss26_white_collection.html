SS26 WHITE COLLECTION

April 2025

This report presents our strategic analysis and recommendations for the SS26 White Collection. By examining Rat & Boa's historical bestsellers, core brand DNA, expansion plans, and direct competitor benchmarking, we've developed a refined range plan that balances growth ambitions with commercial viability.

RANGE COUNT STRATEGY

After careful analysis of historical data, competitive benchmarking, and resource considerations, we recommend refining the collection from the initially proposed 38 SKUs to a more focused 30 SKU approach. This strategic adjustment optimises both growth trajectory and operational efficiency.

Historical Context

The White Collection has seen significant evolution since its inception:

  • 2024 Genesis Collection: 9 SKUs
  • 2025 White Collection: 26 SKUs
  • Proposed 2026 Collection: 30 SKUs

This represents a measured growth trajectory of approximately 15% year-over-year, compared to the more aggressive 46% growth initially proposed with 38 SKUs.

Competitive Benchmark Insights

Analysis of direct competitors reveals that white/bridal collections typically represent 11.1% of a brand's total assortment. With Rat & Boa's current site count at 214 SKUs, our 30-piece collection would represent 14% of total assortment—slightly above industry average but within the competitive range (from 1.0% at Ganni to 16.7% at Bec and Bridge).

Strategic Rationale

Our recommendation to refine the collection to 30 SKUs balances several key considerations:

  • Resource Optimisation: A more focused collection allows design and production resources to concentrate on fewer, higher-quality pieces rather than spreading resources across 38 items simultaneously.
  • Risk Mitigation: With complete sell-through data from the AW25 White Collection not yet available, the 30 SKU approach provides sufficient range while controlling inventory risk.
  • Measured Growth: The 15% YOY growth from 2025 (26 SKUs) to 2026 (30 SKUs) represents a more sustainable expansion compared to the 46% growth that would occur with 38 SKUs.
  • Inventory Investment: The refined collection still allows for category diversification while optimising inventory investment with a more targeted approach.

Implementation Flexibility

While we recommend launching with 30 core SKUs, we've identified an additional 8 reserve SKUs that could be used as potential swap-outs should the brand want to change elements of the collection based on early performance indicators or shifting market trends. This provides flexibility without requiring full development and production of all 38 SKUs simultaneously.

Category Distribution

Our 30-SKU collection is strategically balanced across categories:

  • Dresses (43%): Maintained as the dominant category with emphasis on maxi styles
  • Separates (18%): Strategic selection of pieces that complement the dress assortment, including opportunities to introduce tailoring and leather items
  • Jewelry (21%): Substantial allocation due to strong margins, cross-selling potential, and impressive early production samples
  • Bags (11%): Focused approach with key silhouettes
  • Knitwear (7%): Essential inclusion for styling versatility and layering options

HEADPIECES: OPPORTUNITY CATEGORY

While finalizing our 30-SKU core collection, we identified a significant opportunity to introduce statement headpieces as a strategic category extension for the SS26 White Collection.

Market Gap Analysis

Our competitive analysis revealed a distinctive opportunity: none of our direct competitors currently offer elevated headpieces in their white/bridal collections (outside of wedding veils from Rixo), yet several luxury brands have successfully ventured into this hybrid accessory space.

ortunity

We propose introducing a limited capsule of 2 statement headpieces that would:

  • Fill a distinctive market gap between everyday headbands and full bridal veils
  • Leverage our design expertise in embellishment and hand-finishing techniques
  • Create a truly unique offering compared to our direct competitors
  • Provide compelling editorial styling opportunities for the entire collection
  • Offer excellent margins and gift potential with minimal inventory risk

Price Architecture

The proposal is to introduce one piece in the Better tier, with a Best tier version held in reserve:

Better Tier: £275-325 Mid-range embellishment
Best Tier: £425-475 Premium embellishment

Rat & Boa Concept

Distinctive design with elevated embellishment and hand-finishing

Strategic Positioning Rationale

  • Distinctive Category Positioning: Our proposed headpieces would occupy a unique space between simpler headbands (Pucci at £155) and luxury pieces (Magda Butrym at £705)
  • Brand Elevation: Aligns with Rat & Boa's aspiration to move from lower-mid tier to mid-range positioning, with an exit price for jewelry currently at £500
  • Production Advantage: While not European-made like Magda Butrym's pieces, our elevated designs and quality materials would justify premium positioning
  • Value Perception: The hat/jewelry hybrid concept offers uniqueness in the bridal market, creating perceived value as a statement investment piece and giving customers a reason to shop with Rat & Boa over direct competitors, creating upsell opportunity
