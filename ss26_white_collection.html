SS26 WHITE COLLECTION

April 2025

This report presents our strategic analysis and recommendations for the SS26 White Collection. By examining Rat & Boa's historical bestsellers, core brand DNA, expansion plans, and direct competitor benchmarking, we've developed a refined range plan that balances growth ambitions with commercial viability.

RANGE COUNT STRATEGY

After careful analysis of historical data, competitive benchmarking, and resource considerations, we recommend refining the collection from the initially proposed 38 SKUs to a more focused 30 SKU approach. This strategic adjustment optimizes both growth trajectory and operational efficiency.

Historical Context

The White Collection has seen significant evolution since its inception:

  • 2024 Genesis Collection: 9 SKUs
  • 2025 White Collection: 26 SKUs
  • Proposed 2026 Collection: 30 SKUs

This represents a measured growth trajectory of approximately 15% year-over-year, compared to the more aggressive 46% growth initially proposed with 38 SKUs.

Competitive Benchmark Insights

Analysis of direct competitors reveals that white/bridal collections typically represent 11.1% of a brand's total assortment. With Rat & Boa's current site count at 214 SKUs, our 30-piece collection would represent 14% of total assortment—slightly above industry average but within the competitive range (from 1.0% at Ganni to 16.7% at Bec and Bridge).

Strategic Rationale

Our recommendation to refine the collection to 30 SKUs balances several key considerations:

  • Resource Optimisation: A more focused collection allows design and production resources to concentrate on fewer, higher-quality pieces rather than spreading resources across 38 items simultaneously.
  • Risk Mitigation: With complete sell-through data from the AW25 White Collection not yet available, the 30 SKU approach provides sufficient range while controlling inventory risk.
  • Measured Growth: The 15% YOY growth from 2025 (26 SKUs) to 2026 (30 SKUs) represents a more sustainable expansion compared to the 46% growth that would occur with 38 SKUs.
  • Inventory Investment: The refined collection still allows for category diversification while optimising inventory investment with a more targeted approach.

Implementation Flexibility

While we recommend launching with 30 core SKUs, we've identified an additional 8 reserve SKUs that could be used as potential swap-outs should the brand want to change elements of the collection based on early performance indicators or shifting market trends. This provides flexibility without requiring full development and production of all 38 SKUs simultaneously.

Premium Positioning Evolution

Our 2025 White Collection carried a substantial premium (+74.7%) over our mainline median price—significantly higher than the competitor average (+25.3%). Only Rixo (+61.2%) came close to our premium positioning.

For 2026, we've intentionally moderated this premium to +34.9% by increasing the mainline median price while maintaining the White Collection median at £325, creating a more sustainable pricing architecture while still maintaining a position well above the competitor average.

The Luxury Sweet Spot

This strategic evolution reflects our brand's growing confidence. As our mainline median prices have naturally increased (from £186 to £241), our White Collection doesn't need to maintain the same extreme percentage premium to achieve a luxury positioning.

At £325 median price, we've positioned the collection:

  • Above SIR the Label (+30.0%)
  • Above Bec and Bridge (+15.4%)
  • Alongside the competitor median (£326)
  • With a pricing story that feels special without being inaccessible

Interestingly, Self Portrait takes a unique approach, actually pricing their bridal collection below their mainline (-5.3%)—a reminder that pricing should always tell your brand's unique story rather than following formulas.

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE ASSESSMENT

To ensure our White Collection strategy aligns with market opportunities while maintaining brand distinctiveness, we've analyzed key competitors' design approaches and identified strategic gaps in the market.

Rat & Boa's Core Strengths

Where We Lead:

  • Bias-Cut Expertise: Our signature flowing silhouettes and cowl necklines remain our defining DNA, shared with some competitors but executed with distinctive Rat & Boa flair
  • Open & Low Backs: Our hallmark open-back slip designs offer a sensual yet elegant appeal that resonates with our core customer
  • Strategic Cut-Outs: We're more adventurous with skin-revealing details while maintaining sophistication
  • Lightweight Fabrics: Our expertise with georgette, crinkle chiffon, and draped materials creates ethereal movement
  • Distinctive Prints: Our exclusive prints have become a recognizable signature, similar to Rixo's approach but with our unique aesthetic

Competitive White Market Gaps

Opportunities to Explore:

  • Structured Bodices: Competitors like SIR and Self Portrait offer corseted or underwired cups that provide more bust support
  • Statement Sleeves: Ganni and Self Portrait dominate with puff sleeves and defined shoulders—elements largely absent from our collections
  • Rigid Fabrics: SIR and Reformation feature structured linen and cotton pieces with architectural seaming
  • Waist Definition: Competitors often use belts, smocking, or panel construction to create defined waistlines
  • Modest Options: High necklines and collared designs offer alternative coverage options that we could explore

Strategic Implications

While maintaining our signature bias-cut expertise and sensual aesthetic, the 30-SKU White Collection presents an opportunity to selectively incorporate design elements that broaden our appeal:

  • Balanced Innovation: Introduce 3-4 pieces with more structured elements (underwire or corseting) while preserving our fluid DNA in the majority of the collection
  • Complementary Categories: Utilise accessories (particularly jewelry and bags) to enhance our core styles rather than dramatically shifting our apparel approach
  • Material Exploration: Experiment with structured fabrics in our signature silhouettes to bridge the gap between our aesthetic and the more architectural pieces offered by competitors
  • Increased Modesty Features: Consider non-sheer fabrics and more coverage options to cast a wider net and capture additional market share beyond our core customer

This strategic approach allows us to maintain our distinctive identity while thoughtfully expanding our appeal to capture white/bridal customers seeking both our signature fluidity and occasional structure.
