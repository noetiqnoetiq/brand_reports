SS26 WHITE COLLECTION

April 2025

This report presents our strategic analysis and recommendations for the SS26 White Collection. By examining Rat & Boa's historical bestsellers, core brand DNA, expansion plans, and direct competitor benchmarking, we've developed a refined range plan that balances growth ambitions with commercial viability.

RANGE COUNT STRATEGY

After careful analysis of historical data, competitive benchmarking, and resource considerations, we recommend refining the collection from the initially proposed 38 SKUs to a more focused 30 SKU approach. This strategic adjustment optimizes both growth trajectory and operational efficiency.

Historical Context

The White Collection has seen significant evolution since its inception:

  • 2024 Genesis Collection: 9 SKUs
  • 2025 White Collection: 26 SKUs
  • Proposed 2026 Collection: 30 SKUs

This represents a measured growth trajectory of approximately 15% year-over-year, compared to the more aggressive 46% growth initially proposed with 38 SKUs.

Competitive Benchmark Insights

Analysis of direct competitors reveals that white/bridal collections typically represent 11.1% of a brand's total assortment. With Rat & Boa's current site count at 214 SKUs, our 30-piece collection would represent 14% of total assortment—slightly above industry average but within the competitive range (from 1.0% at Ganni to 16.7% at Bec and Bridge).

Strategic Rationale

Our recommendation to refine the collection to 30 SKUs balances several key considerations:

  • Resource Optimisation: A more focused collection allows design and production resources to concentrate on fewer, higher-quality pieces rather than spreading resources across 38 items simultaneously.
  • Risk Mitigation: With complete sell-through data from the AW25 White Collection not yet available, the 30 SKU approach provides sufficient range while controlling inventory risk.
  • Measured Growth: The 15% YOY growth from 2025 (26 SKUs) to 2026 (30 SKUs) represents a more sustainable expansion compared to the 46% growth that would occur with 38 SKUs.
  • Inventory Investment: The refined collection still allows for category diversification while optimising inventory investment with a more targeted approach.

Implementation Flexibility

While we recommend launching with 30 core SKUs, we've identified an additional 8 reserve SKUs that could be activated based on early performance indicators. This phased approach provides both structure and flexibility, allowing Rat & Boa to optimise inventory investment while maintaining agility.

CATEGORY MIX STRATEGY

Our 30 SKU recommendation for the SS26 White Collection features a carefully balanced category mix that both respects market norms and leverages Rat & Boa's unique strengths. This approach optimises commercial potential while maintaining the brand's distinctive DNA.

Category Distribution

The collection's category breakdown intentionally overweights dresses—Rat & Boa's signature category—while maintaining strategic representation across complementary categories:

Strategic Category Insights

Our category mix intentionally diverges from market averages to emphasise Rat & Boa's strengths while strategically expanding into complementary categories. Key insights include:

  • Dress Dominance: At 43.3% (vs. market average of 20.8%), our dress-forward strategy maintains Rat & Boa's signature positioning while direct competitors like Réalisation Par and Rixo show greater category diversification.
  • Jewelry Emphasis: At 20% of the mix (vs. market average of 10.4%), we've prioritised jewelry as a key accessory category, outpacing direct competitors like Self Portrait while providing strong margin opportunity.
  • Considered Knitwear Approach: With an intentionally modest 6.7% representation (vs. market average of 14.6%), our approach to knitwear is measured, allowing the brand to test the category without overcommitting inventory.

Dress Length Strategy

Within the dress assortment, we've developed a length strategy that emphasises Rat & Boa's signature maxi silhouettes while ensuring representation across key lengths:

  • Maxi Dresses: 8 SKUs (61.5%)
  • Midi Dresses: 3 SKUs (23.1%)
  • Mini Dresses: 2 SKUs (15.4%)

This distribution recognises the brand's historical success with maxi silhouettes while building presence in the growing midi category, particularly in the Better price segment.

Price Segmentation

The collection's price architecture creates a balanced good-better-best segmentation with a strategic emphasis on the Better segment:

  • Good: 9 SKUs (30.0%)
  • Better: 15 SKUs (50.0%)
  • Best: 6 SKUs (20.0%)

This intentional weighting towards the middle Better segment allows for accessibility at entry price points while providing opportunity to elevate average order value through the sizeable Better assortment.
