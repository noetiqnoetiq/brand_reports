STRATEGIC ANALYSIS DOSSIER (DRESSES)

February 2025

1. Opening Summary

Rat & Boa ("R&B") is positioned in the Lower-Mid price tier (average £245), alongside brands like Bec & Bridge (£255) and House of CB (£290). Having identified margin constraints that currently limit wholesale expansion, the company plans to strategically elevate its market position towards the Mid-Tier segment. This will be achieved by introducing new core bestsellers with improved margins and gradually raising the Weighted Average Price (WAP). This evolution will create a more omnichannel-ready business model while preserving the label's signature DNA and maintaining strategic entry-level price points to protect core revenue streams.

2. Key Observations from Data

Signature Prints & Sheer Fabric

Rat & Boa's best sellers—e.g. Valentina, Fiorella, Athena—center on bold prints, semi-sheer finishes, and bias-cut silhouettes. These elements define the brand's seductive appeal.

Fabrics & Support Features

  • Cotton: Approximately 90% of competitor brands incorporate cotton dresses, yielding hundreds of relevant SKUs. Our analysis shows 694 competitor cotton dresses—a stark difference from R&B's near-absence of cotton styles.
  • Bra-Friendly & Structured: About 9% of competitor dresses explicitly highlight "bra-friendly," while numerous brands offer underwire or boning.
  • Modesty & Coverage: Over half of competitor brands provide modest attributes through sleeve variations, bra-friendly designs, and additional coverage options. Key examples include Proenza Schouler (46% of range with short sleeves), Bec & Bridge (46% with bra-friendly designs), and Toteme (13% featuring cap sleeves).

3. Over-Indexed Key Attributes & USP

Beyond competitor gaps, Rat & Boa also leads in several style attributes, particularly Maxi lengths, Printed styles, and Sheer finishes. These features are core to R&B’s identity and present a strong USP—but they also carry risk if consumer preferences evolve away from these areas or if R&B relies too heavily on a narrow offering.

4. Key Rationale for Broadening the Range

  • Untapped Consumer Segments: Bust support, modest coverage, and more accessible fabrications enable R&B to cater to those who love the brand's style but find current pieces too revealing or impractical.
  • Higher Margins & Brand Elevation: Introducing dress styles in the £335–£495 range—or even £550+—creates room for margin growth and ensures R&B looks and feels more premium.
  • Maintaining DNA with Added Elements: R&B's trademark bias cuts, Maxi Length, cowl necklines, and vivid prints remain at the core; the opportunity lies in enhancing the assortment to capture more market share currently dominated by competitors with more balanced offerings.

5. Detailed Opportunities

(1) Bra-Friendly & Bust Support

Competitor data indicates around 9% explicit bra-friendly usage, plus widespread underwire/boning from major labels. Rat & Boa adopting carefully integrated support features can command higher prices and accommodate a broader audience.

(2) Cotton & Daywear

With around 694 competitor cotton dresses on record, the market heavily embraces cotton dresses while R&B doesn't, missing out on this popular casual and breathable option.

(3) Structured Silhouettes

Underwire, boning, or adjustable shaping can evolve R&B's slip dress heritage into formal or supportive lines—often priced at the upper end.

(4) Sleeves & Coverage

Analysis shows R&B's adoption of coverage attributes through maxi lengths (59 SKUs) and long sleeves (24 SKUs), with sheer styles indexing significantly higher than market (37.38% vs 4.85%). Additional modesty attributes through strategic design elements include:

  • Additional sleeve varieties to complement current strap and long sleeve offering
  • Enhanced coverage options
  • Bra-friendly construction
  • Lined fabric alternatives (current adoption 19.63% vs market 27.01%)
  • Adjustable straps (current offering 25.23% of range)

6. Conclusion

Rat & Boa has significant opportunities to elevate its market position through strategic price increases and expanded market reach. By raising its weighted average price point while diversifying into new segments, the brand can attract both higher-spending customers and a broader consumer base seeking more versatile options.

The new data shows R&B over-indexes on Maxi lengths, Printed styles, and Sheer finishes — a key differentiator that can be leveraged as a USP. However, R&B must carefully manage its product mix to avoid over-reliance on any single attribute and continue addressing the market’s demand for modesty, bra-friendly support, and more inclusive designs.

Ultimately, this evolution will boost margins, build a wholesale-friendly business model, and help transition R&B from the lower-mid segment to a true mid-tier brand, while maintaining its core identity and capitalising on the competitive gaps highlighted in this analysis.
