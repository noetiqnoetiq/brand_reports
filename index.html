RAT & BOA STRATEGIC ANALYSIS DOSSIER (DRESSES)

February 2025

1. Opening Summary

Rat & Boa ("R&B") is positioned in the Lower-Mid price tier (average £245), alongside brands like Bec & Bridge (£255) and House of CB (£290). The company intends to incrementally elevate R&B's market position towards the Mid-Tier segment in the future, aiming to raise the Weighted Average Price (WAP) and product offering accordingly, while preserving the label's signature DNA and safeguarding its core revenue at entry-level price points.

2. Key Observations from Data

Signature Prints & Sheer Fabric

Rat & Boa's best sellers—e.g. Valentina, Fiorella, Athena—center on bold prints, semi-sheer finishes, and bias-cut silhouettes. These elements define the brand's seductive appeal.

Fabrics & Support Features

  • Cotton: Approximately 90% of competitor brands incorporate cotton dresses, yielding hundreds of relevant SKUs. Our analysis shows 694 competitor cotton dresses—a stark difference from R&B's near-absence of cotton styles. Market leaders in cotton adoption include Pucci (64% of range), COS (54% of range), and Tropic of C (50% of range).
  • Bra-Friendly & Structured: About 9% of competitor dresses explicitly highlight "bra-friendly," while numerous brands offer underwire or boning. Notable leaders include Clio Peppiatt (100% of range includes support features), Hunza G (90% of range), and House of CB (16% of range). For specific bust support, Hunza G leads with 60% of their range.
  • Modesty & Coverage: Over half of competitor brands provide modest attributes through sleeve variations, bra-friendly designs, and additional coverage options. Key examples include Proenza Schouler (46% of range with short sleeves), Bec & Bridge (46% with bra-friendly designs), and Toteme (13% featuring cap sleeves).

3. Key Rationale for Broadening the Range

  • Untapped Consumer Segments: Bust support, modest coverage, and more accessible fabrications enable R&B to cater to those who love the brand's style but find current pieces too revealing or impractical.
  • Higher Margins & Brand Elevation: Introducing dress styles in the £335–£495 range—or even £550+—creates room for margin growth and ensures R&B looks and feels more premium.
  • Maintaining DNA with Added Elements: R&B's trademark bias cuts, cowl necklines, and vivid prints remain at the core; the difference lies in enhancing them with coverage, structural details, and new fabric types.

4. Detailed Opportunities

(1) Bra-Friendly & Bust Support

Competitor data indicates around 9% explicit bra-friendly usage, plus widespread underwire/boning from major labels. Rat & Boa adopting carefully integrated support features can command higher prices and accommodate a broader audience.

(2) Cotton & Daywear

With around 694 competitor cotton dresses on record, the market heavily embraces breathable, less formal options—an area R&B largely misses.

(3) Structured Silhouettes

Underwire, boning, or adjustable shaping can evolve R&B's slip dress heritage into formal or supportive lines—often priced at the upper end.

(4) Sleeves & Coverage

Analysis reveals that over half of competitor brands incorporate modest attributes through strategic design elements including:

  • Varied sleeve lengths (short/cap/long)
  • Enhanced coverage options
  • Bra-friendly construction
  • Lined fabric alternatives
  • Adjustable coverage features

5. Conclusion

By addressing coverage, bust support, and cotton daywear—all supported by competitor data—Rat & Boa can confidently transition from Lower-Mid to a Mid-Tier standing. The essence of the brand remains, but the product line broadens to serve a wider audience, capturing:

  • Under-served segments: coverage, modesty, bra-friendly fits
  • Higher price points & margins: fueling brand evolution
  • Enhanced market positioning: more inclusive, more premium, and better poised for wholesale expansion
